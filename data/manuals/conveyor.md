# Conveyor Belt Maintenance Guide

## Motor Issues
- **Overheating:** If the conveyor motor feels excessively hot or smells like burning plastic, immediately cut power. This is usually caused by excessive load on the belt, inadequate ventilation around the motor, or worn internal bearings.
- **Sparking:** Sparking from the motor housing indicates worn brushes (on DC motors) or a severe electrical short. Lock out/tag out the equipment and call an electrical technician. Do not attempt to restart.

## Belt Alignment and Tracking
If the belt is drifting to one side:
1. Inspect the rollers and idlers for buildup of material. Clean them using the approved heavy-duty solvent.
2. Adjust the tension on the tail pulley. If the belt is tracking to the left, tighten the tension on the left side (or loosen the right side) slightly.
3. Make small adjustments and let the belt run for a few full rotations to observe the effect.

## Belt Tension
- **Slipping:** If the drive pulley turns but the belt slips, the tension is too low. Tighten the take-up screws evenly on both sides.
- **Excessive Wear on Edges:** Usually caused by poor tracking rubbing against the conveyor frame.
