# HVAC Troubleshooting Guide

## Air Conditioning Unit Cooling Issue
If the AC unit is not cooling:
1. Check the thermostat settings to ensure it is set to "Cool" and the temperature is lower than the room temperature.
2. Inspect the air filter. A dirty filter restricts airflow. Replace it if it looks clogged.
3. Examine the condenser coils outside. Remove any debris, leaves, or dirt blocking the fins.
4. Check the refrigerant levels. Low refrigerant often indicates a leak that requires a certified technician to repair.

## Unusual Noises
- **Squealing:** Typically indicates a worn or loose blower belt. Replace or tighten as necessary.
- **Banging or Clanking:** Indicates a loose or broken part, such as a fan blade or motor mount. Shut down immediately.
- **Hissing:** Could indicate a refrigerant leak or a severe duct leak.

## Routine Maintenance
- Replace air filters every 1 to 3 months.
- Clean the evaporator and condenser coils annually.
- Inspect and clean the condensate drain line to prevent water damage.
